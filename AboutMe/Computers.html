About Grönis; Computers

I like to play games at my PC, and that is one of the most frequent activities. Other activities are 3D rendering, photo manipulation, and mixing music.

I'm extremely picky about hardware when it comes to serious gaming. Most of my friends would certainly agree with this. This means I spend a lot of money on fast graphics-card and so on. I have trouble seeing the detail level as other people see, so I go for speed instead. I want my games to react quick to my movements with the joystick and mouse.

My 3D rendering period started at the university. I found a program called vivid. This was a primitive program that you had to actually program to get it to render any image.
The programming language resembles very much to Turbo Pascal and C. You could not create holes in the surfaces, you could only add surfaces in it. At least I think so. I never found any commands of how to remove a section of any object.
To make a "preview" you rendered the entire image, in a smaller scale. This was back in the days when Intel's 80486 was the fastest one. It took around 5 hours for my huge images (1024x768) to render. I had at most 20 objects (spheres, boxes).... Those where the days. After creating the output file, you had to convert it to .gif. Ray tracing in 256 colors was great back then.

Back in those days, I normally listened to Laser Dance when creating the images.

I've always been interested in sound on the computer. My first file that I ever sampled was KLFs "3 AM Eternal". I sampled this through the SoundBlaster PRO, which had stereo. It got 5 floppy disks big. Right now my music files are a little bit more advanced....

The type of games I play are normally racing games. To give you an example. MicroProse Formula One Grand Prix 1, I completed at the hardest level. There where more than 12 races, and I drove on the hardest level, full race length. Each race took then more than 2 hours. If I didn't win I had to race the track again. Needless to say, this took some time to complete.
Some of the good games are: Quake III Arena, Rally Masters, STCC 2.

Most fun is to play racing (car/bike) and shooting games (Quake III Team Arena) in a network with many people in one house.
