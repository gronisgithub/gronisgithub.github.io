Icehotel, Jukkasjärvi, Sweden, 2001-02

 


























 

  


 

 I felt like getting really cold, so why not spend the night in an Ice hotel? Well, if you know me well enough, you know that I don't really like cold. But I like snow and ice, and snow often requires cold temperatures. This is the coolest snow and ice creation I've heard of, so I decided I wanted to go there. The hotel is located in a very small village near Kiruna, northern Sweden.


All I really knew before I went there was that I should bring warm clothes (-5 degrees inside), and some thing thin to sleep in (inside a sleeping bag). I also wanted to see the northern lights, but no such luck this time. But, let's not waste any time. Let's enter the hotel, and see what can be expected.
As can be seen on this picture the hotel is not 100% ice and snow, but close enough. A few doors, and some lights and so on.


 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 


Inside the hotel you encounter many different Ice sculptures. Most of them have some light source so it's easier to see them after sundown. Inside the hotel it is bright enough, but not as bright as you are used to in your home.
It was quite tricky to take photos here, as I was not sure how the flash would affect the pictures. After looking at the end result, most of them where best after using the flash, and shot for 3-5 seconds.


The hotel has it's own sauna. Actually 2 versions: 1 normal one, and 1 of Ice, as seen on this picture. As you see some sculptures, like the above, are not so elaborate as these "rooms". You could find several beautiful sculptures, and these are a few of them.


As you see, it's the sets of sculptures that caught my eye at first. But after a while, the feeling of actually walking in a hotel made of snow and ice became the great feeling that kept me happy inside the hotel.




A few lamps, but the rest of the wall is actually snow. Some ice pillars in the middle of the alley, but I wonder if they really have any effect on the stability of the walls. The ice is just as clear in these pillars, as in any other part of the building.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 


This table with the chairs and the lamp, located in the center of the hotel, caught most peoples eyes. This is the coldest chair I've ever sat on, but also the most exquisite. You couldn't sit there for long.

If you instead chose a room to spend your night in, this is what they look like. If you are able to sleep in the ice hotel, you will get a small diploma.

A bit of advice: Make sure you do not have a cold, or feel sick when going here. The cold air will certainly not help you during night (as I found out the hard way).

Would I recommend anyone to go here? Yes, if you are not afraid of the temperature outside (even colder than the indoor -5 degrees).
 


Sleeping in the IceHotel is also an awesome experience.
