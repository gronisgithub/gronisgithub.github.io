Italy, Bologna, 2004-06.

I can't say that I have been too long in Bologna, but I can say that most of the time spent in Bologna was in the Ducati factory and Museum.

The guided tour of the factory is in English, and you can actually see them assembling the bikes there. That's where the good parts stops.
The guide speaks very low, so you have to be in the forward 10% of the group to hear what she says. And the tour is quite boring, as all parts are 3rd party products, and just assembled here. No photos are allowed in there.

During my visit, I didn't se a single yellow 999 in the factory, making mine quite unique. Hurray.

Troy Corsers bike that he used in 96. The rear wheel even has the T.Corser logo on it.

After the tour you visit the museum which is quite nice for 10 minutes.

Then you are allowed to buy souvenirs in the local shop. It's even more expensive in this shop, than the local Ducati dealer in Sweden.

If it wasn't for these bikes, I would have been very disappointed with the day, as we drove here only to visit the Ducati factory.

This is the bike that Foggy won with in 2002 and 2003, and It's very nice to have seen it.

If you are thinking of impressing Ducati with driving to the factory with your (Ducati?) bike: Don't. They will not be impressed with anything but your wallet.
