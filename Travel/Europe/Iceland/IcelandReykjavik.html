Iceland, Reykjavik, 2001-03.

My dream has come true. This was one of the countries I really wished that I could visit. The language here is very beautiful, and the Icelandic singer Björk uses her native language in some songs. This was the second thing that made me want to go here. The first thing was the nature. I wasn't let down in any way by Iceland. It was better than I expected. Much better.

At first look at this picture, this could have been shot in Switzerland, just by a lake. However, this is not a lake, and very much colder than in Switzerland. The car in the center of the picture is also very typical for Reykjavik; Extremely big, and some of the largest wheels I've ever seen on a car that can drive in the city.

On Iceland, you have a mix of religions. Christianity, and the old Nordic mythology.

The beach of Reykjavik. I guess you don't go here for the nice white beaches, but rather for the black lava-rocks that will act as the beach. As Reykjavik is located in the coast of Iceland, you get a lot of seafood to choose from. Lamb is also a very "common" dish, since they don't have to import lamb. I'm really curious if they make Hamburgers or lamb burgers.

If you take a look at this sculpture from another angle you will see that this looks like a Viking ship.

Bank (Banki) and Post office (Postur) on the opposite sides of the street.

I would say that this sums up quite much of how Icelandic is compared to Swedish; Just add 'i' or 'ur' to the names, and you could very easily be correct.

The city, and the road is leading up to Hallgrimskirkja.

The nature is not co colorific, so they tend to create everything man made in clear colours.

The not so small church acting as a landmark. To give you an impression of the height, take a look at the next picture and compare this to the window that you see in both pictures.

Inside the church Hallgrimskirkja. The Icelandic people where originally Vikings, so it's a quite young country. The religion of the Vikings is quite different. They believed that if you died in battle, you would go to their form of heaven (Valhalla). In there you fought during the whole day, and partied at night. If you where killed during the daily battle in Valhalla, you would be resurrected the next day, and you would go on fighting. This is where every Viking wanted to go, so he practiced to be good at fighting as much as he could, when he was alive.

A statue of the Viking that founded Iceland.

The most typical of all houses; white walls and green (or red) roof.

This company one amused me much. "Odyrt Bensin" - "Non-expensive Petrol". Why not have the name "Cheap Gas" instead? But if they would have had that name, I would not have noticed it that much.
