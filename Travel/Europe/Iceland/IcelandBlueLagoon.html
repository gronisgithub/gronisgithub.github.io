Iceland, The Blue Lagoon, 2001-03.


The blue lagoon. Nice place to take a hot bath in. As the water is geothermal, it will stay hot. Thankfully it's cold enough for us to enter. The water here has lots of minerals. When you get out of the bath, and after you've showered, your skin feels very strange. Very soft, and the minerals are very hard to get rid of.

As you see, the water is quite warm, and filled with these white minerals.

The closer you went towards this spring, the warmer the water got. I had to stop some 10 meters away, as it got too hot then.

This lava stone is what the bottom of the blue lagoon is made of. But fortunately a lot smoother. You still have to watch your step anyway, or you might cut yourself on the rocks like I did. But bathing outside, when the air is +4 degrees feels strange. But if you stay in the water all the time you will be too hot, so you have to go up to cool yourself. Imagine doing that in Thailand or in Singapore.
