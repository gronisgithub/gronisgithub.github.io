Israel, Tel Aviv, 1999-01

Tel Aviv is a very busy, high tech city. I always assumed that they only had ruins and guns there. Shows how much I know, doesn't it?

Whenever you're there, you should go to the lookout point. This enables you to see all over the great city, and on a clear night it really astounds you. Most lookout points do, I agree, but this one is definitely better than the normal ones.

The beach is also very good, and can be recommended. If you are from a cold area (like Sweden), you will have no problem swimming in the ocean during winter. It's hot enough.

The inhabitants tend to go out for dinner a lot later than the other places I've been to. To them 11 PM seems to be the normal time go to dinner, and the places are packed with people. I don't know how they manage. Totally the opposite of the US. I can't really tell if the local food is good or not, since I did not dare to try it. Stupid me. I have myself to blame.

One thing I was jealous of was that they had fresh strawberries at winter, and we normally have strawberries during midsummer. As you see, I was not completely wrong when I told you that you can go swimming in winter in Tel Aviv.

When we where out one night, we started to talk about the differences between Israeli and Swedish people. Again they said that Swedish people are very calm, and listens patiently to other people. I guess that I have to take their word for it.

They told us that it is the law to have Air-condition in your car. Presumably they have had too many accidents because people are tired after work, and it gets worse if you sit in a hot car. All you want to do is get home as fast as possible, and your temper rises with the temperature. So the accidents have dropped since they all have the possibility to have a nice cool car.
Another strange thing is that the police cars has to drive with their lights on all the time. So how do you know if you're being pulled over?
