India, Kanataka, 2003-02


This is a very local thing. Sugar cane juice. All they do to produce the sugar cane juice is to insert a few canes in the press, and out comes some fluid.

On our way to watch the tigers, we had to go on a very local train. It has the cheap way of air-condition: Old reliable fans, open doors and windows. The seats are quite small. 3 people on seats that we would think are designed for 2.

The guy, in the blue shirt on the lower left part of the picture, didn't seem to have seen any European before. He and his friends where constantly staring at us for 4 hours. The last half hour of the train, they played card, and didn't stare so much at us then.

But one of the guys in that group was standing and looking at us with a pair of binoculars. As you see, it's not very far away...

After about 24 hours of travel, we finally came to the tigers. The national park is called "Teverekopa" or similar.

What a disappointment. They where kept in cages (no roof on the cage, but anyhow), measuring 500m x 1000m. The deer (tiger food) where kept a large distance away from the tigers.

So 48 hours of traveling for 20 minutes of tigers in cages was not what I expected, when I signed up for the "Tiger tour".

And to add to the damage, I accidentally overwrote 250 of my photos. On those photos where where I got the great photos of the tigers. So not even these I got home with me.

All in all: a very disappointing trip.

Female lion in another cage, resting in the shadow (just like tigers was).

On our way back from watching tigers in a cage, Strikers where stopping the train. At first I got really scared, because they seemed very upset and violent. However, they felt happy with them selves, just by stopping the train. So there was no actual danger. Lucky for me.

They where striking against that the price of Diesel had gone up.

Around lunchtime, when it was extremely hot, the railway workers where taking a break.

But when they didn't have a break, they where working very very close to the train. The train slowed down to around 30km/h, but the workers where still hacking in the ground, only a meter from the rail. Not very safe, but it's their ways.

A railway worker makes around 150 rupees per day, for their hard work. This is equivalent to €3/day.

1½ liters of coca cola costs 35 rupees.

Whenever we saw a river, there was always much life (compared to everywhere else).

With the river, came green trees, and the possibility to grow crops. So there where always Green trees, People with houses and farms, animals, cows, close to the rivers.

Also fishing was a big part of generating food and income to the family. I was surprised how much fish was in these dirty rivers. Very small fish (7 cm maximum), but still fish that the locals eat.

Another strike, and people stopping the train, in protest of the raising diesel price.

The soil was always very red in Goa, as there is very much iron in the soil.
