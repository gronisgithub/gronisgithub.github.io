China, Beijing, The Great Wall close to Si Ma Tai, 2001-08

Last time I visited Ba Da Ling, and it was packed of tourists. This part of the wall is so much better, and you can climb a lot more. For those that doesn't want to climb, there is a shortcut that costs some money. But for us that really want to experience, you have a 2-3 hour climb, and ½-1 hour back down.
 

Let's start our walk to see as much as we can of The Great Wall, here close to Si Ma Tai. We start of at the base of a lake.

I can see The Great Wall on the top here. Some walk we have to do.

And for every group of tourists, a Chinese person will accompany you. They will sell Water (which will come in handy), or many useless stuff. When you come up higher, you will find some other places where they sell water. It's cheaper to buy them at the tourist shops at the base. But, then you will have to carry the water for quite a while.

You will need lots of water.

The Great Wall is disappearing in the mist.

It's HUGE. I can't really understand that it stretches all the way to Ba Da Ling. And that's only a small part of it.

It is truly the only wonder of the world, still standing.
 
A nice part of the wall. And it just goes up, up, and up.

Hey, it goes down for a short while. Very scenic part of the wall.

If you remember the lake from the first picture, then you can get an impression of the walk you have to make. Nice scenery up here.

Some places you where actually looking down on some mountain ridges.

At some parts, The Great Wall doesn't have anything to block you from falling out.

Luckily we where here when it was misty, so there was almost no wind.

Come to think of it, I was quite warm at this point, Perhaps some wind would cool me off.



Some part of the wall that you have climb to get to better parts, look like this. This is all that's left of the wall right here.
